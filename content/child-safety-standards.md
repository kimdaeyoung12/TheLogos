# ChristianDays Child Safety Standards

**Last updated:** June 13, 2026

ChristianDays is a faith-based community app for sharing posts, comments, prayers, and community discussions. We do not tolerate child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE), child sexual abuse material (CSAM), grooming, sexual solicitation of minors, trafficking, or any behavior that exploits, harms, or endangers children.

These standards apply to all ChristianDays users, posts, comments, profiles, links, media, and community interactions.

## 1. Zero tolerance for CSAE and CSAM

ChristianDays prohibits any content or conduct involving:

- Child sexual abuse material (CSAM), including real, computer-generated, illustrated, or altered sexual content involving minors.
- Sexualization, grooming, sexual solicitation, coercion, blackmail, or exploitation of minors.
- Attempts to move conversations with minors to unsafe external channels for sexual, exploitative, or abusive purposes.
- Sharing, requesting, linking to, describing how to obtain, or encouraging access to CSAM or CSAE-related content.
- Content that normalizes, praises, jokes about, or minimizes child sexual abuse or exploitation.

Accounts or content involved in these behaviors may be removed or restricted without notice.

## 2. Reporting and in-app safety tools

Users can report posts, comments, or accounts through the in-app reporting feature. Reports may include a reason and additional details so our team can review the issue.

For urgent child safety concerns or cases where the in-app report flow is not available, contact us directly:

- **Child safety contact:** [support@christiandays.app](mailto:support@christiandays.app)

Please include enough information for review, such as the post, comment, username, screenshot, or other context. Do not send or forward CSAM itself unless specifically required by a lawful authority.

## 3. Review and enforcement

When we receive a child safety report, we review the reported content or account and take appropriate action. Depending on the severity and evidence, actions may include:

- Removing violating content.
- Restricting, suspending, or terminating accounts.
- Preserving relevant information when legally required.
- Reporting apparent CSAM or imminent child safety threats to appropriate authorities where required by law.

We may also take action against users who submit false, malicious, or abusive reports.

## 4. External links and community content

ChristianDays prohibits using links, previews, usernames, profile text, posts, comments, or community channels to promote or distribute CSAE or CSAM. This includes links to third-party websites, messaging groups, cloud storage, or social media accounts that host or facilitate child exploitation.

## 5. Legal compliance

ChristianDays is committed to complying with applicable child safety laws and with Google Play's child safety standards policy. We cooperate with lawful requests from relevant authorities and may update these standards as legal, platform, or safety requirements change.
